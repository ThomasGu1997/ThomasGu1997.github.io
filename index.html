About

Turning messy data into defensible decisions.

I'm an engineering Ph.D. researcher at McMaster University, working at the intersection of machine learning, optimization, and simulation for intelligent transportation systems. My research develops predictive models and optimization algorithms, then validates them the way a scientist should — benchmarking against strong baselines, cross-validating, and confirming results hold up before they're trusted.

I'm the author of six peer-reviewed publications and a peer reviewer for journals including Transportation Research Part A, Transportmetrica A, and IEEE Access. I complete my Ph.D. in 2026 and am available to start in September 2026.

Research

What I work on

Machine learning and operations research applied to real, noisy, high-stakes systems — with the flagship results below.

Optimization & Operations Research

Exact and metaheuristic optimization for decision-making under constraints. My flagship method, QISTO (Queue-Informed Signal Timing Optimization), cut average delay by 72% versus a throughput-optimal baseline at under 3.1% throughput cost, while pruning 62% of the search space — first-author, under review at Transportation Research Part B.

Combinatorial optimizationGA · PSO · TabuVRP / VRPTW

Machine Learning & Predictive Modeling

Building and benchmarking models on large, real-world data. A deep-learning + neuro-fuzzy (ANFIS) framework in TensorFlow/Keras estimated queue lengths to 1.65–9.25% MAPE; a random forest reached 0.83 accuracy / 0.825 AUC as the best of four benchmarked algorithms.

Deep learningClassificationANFIS

Anomaly Detection & Screening

Detecting rare, irregular patterns in noisy operational data — headway anomaly detection for lane-based queuing-status classification, published in Transportation Research Record (2025). The same discipline transfers directly to fraud- and risk-screening problems.

Anomaly detectionStatistical validation

Simulation & Quantitative Analysis

Microscopic traffic simulation (SUMO, VISSIM) calibrated to field data, end-to-end analysis pipelines automated in Python, and rigorous validation — k-fold cross-validation, hypothesis testing, and head-to-head benchmarking to keep results reproducible.
