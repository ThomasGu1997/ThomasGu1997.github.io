Research in Progress · Ph.D., McMaster University

Network-Scale Signal Control under Oversaturation

Extending queue-aware signal optimization from a single corridor to a congested urban network. A fixed plan tuned to be optimal on one corridor can degrade the whole network when it is deployed everywhere. This work names that gap the price of local optimality. The remedy recasts the queue-aware objective as an adaptive control law that re-decides every few seconds, with an optional perimeter gate that solves a capacity-constrained admission linear program to meter inflow at the network boundary. Evaluated at network scale in microsimulation against strong adaptive baselines.

First-author manuscript in preparation

Adaptive signal controlPerimeter gatingNetwork optimization

Network-Scale Conflict-Risk Prediction

Predicting where rear-end conflict risk will concentrate across a signalized network, one cycle ahead, from the sparse stopline detectors intersections already have. Congestion blinds those detectors exactly where risk builds. A spatiotemporal graph neural network therefore treats every road cell, observed or not, as a node in a typed directed graph and infers risk in the blind spots. The risk fields are built and validated entirely in SUMO microsimulation, against simulated collisions never used in training. The work extends my queue-estimation and queuing-status studies from traffic state to safety.

Flagship Results · Ph.D., McMaster University

Optimization & Sequential Decision-Making

Optimizing operational decisions under hard constraints. My flagship Queue-Aware Signal Timing Optimization (QASTO) method re-optimizes an operational control plan cycle after cycle from live queue information — reducing average delay by 72% versus a throughput-optimal baseline while matching its throughput within 3.1%, and pruning 62% of the candidate solution space.

First-author manuscript under review · Transportation Research Part B: Methodological

Constrained optimizationSearch-space pruningMetaheuristics

Machine Learning & Estimation

Building and benchmarking estimation models on real-world data. A deep-learning + neuro-fuzzy (ANFIS) framework in TensorFlow/Keras estimated cycle-by-cycle queue lengths to 1.65–9.25% MAPE, with a compact MLP outperforming a heavier CNN-LSTM-attention benchmark (IEEE Access, 2024).

Deep learningEstimationANFIS

Anomaly Detection & Screening

Detecting rare, irregular patterns in noisy operational data — headway anomaly detection for lane-based queuing-status classification, where a random forest won a four-algorithm benchmark at 0.83 accuracy / 0.918 AUC (Transportation Research Record, 2025). The same discipline transfers directly to fraud- and risk-screening problems.

Anomaly detectionStatistical validation

High-fidelity microsimulation (SUMO, VISSIM) calibrated to field data — a testbed for the decision algorithms I build — with end-to-end analysis pipelines automated in Python and rigorous validation: k-fold cross-validation, hypothesis testing, and head-to-head benchmarking to keep results reproducible.
