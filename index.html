About

Turning messy data into defensible decisions.

I'm an engineering Ph.D. researcher at McMaster University, working at the intersection of machine learning, optimization, and simulation. My research turns noisy sensor data into reliable estimates, and estimates into operational decisions — validating every step: benchmarking against strong baselines, cross-validating, and confirming results hold up before they're trusted. Intelligent transportation is my proving ground; the methods generalize.

I'm the author of peer-reviewed journal publications and a peer reviewer for journals including Transportation Research Part A, Transportmetrica A, and IEEE Access. I expect to complete my Ph.D. in 2026.

Research

What I work on

Machine learning and operations research applied to real, noisy, high-stakes systems — with the flagship results below.

Optimization & Sequential Decision-Making

Optimizing operational decisions under hard constraints. My flagship Queue-Informed Signal Timing Optimization (QISTO) method re-optimizes an operational control plan cycle after cycle from live queue information — reducing average delay by 72% versus a throughput-optimal baseline while matching its throughput within 3.1%, and pruning 62% of the candidate solution space.

First-author manuscript under review · Transportation Research Part B: Methodological

Constrained optimizationSearch-space pruningMetaheuristics

Machine Learning & Predictive Modeling

Building and benchmarking models on real-world data. A deep-learning + neuro-fuzzy (ANFIS) framework in TensorFlow/Keras estimated queue lengths to 1.65–9.25% MAPE; a random forest reached 0.83 accuracy / 0.825 AUC as the best of four benchmarked algorithms.

Deep learningClassificationANFIS

Anomaly Detection & Screening

Detecting rare, irregular patterns in noisy operational data — headway anomaly detection for lane-based queuing-status classification, published in Transportation Research Record (2025). The same discipline transfers directly to fraud- and risk-screening problems.

Anomaly detectionStatistical validation

Simulation & Quantitative Analysis

High-fidelity microsimulation (SUMO, VISSIM) calibrated to field data — a testbed for the decision algorithms I build — with end-to-end analysis pipelines automated in Python and rigorous validation: k-fold cross-validation, hypothesis testing, and head-to-head benchmarking to keep results reproducible.

SUMO · VISSIMPython pipelinesk-fold CV

Routing & Network Optimization

Earlier work · M.Phil., Hohai University (2019–2022)

Metaheuristic vehicle routing (VRP/VRPTW; genetic algorithms, improved particle swarm optimization) for metro-integrated urban logistics — emergency medical-supplies delivery (Transportation Research Record, 2024) and single-metro-line delivery planning (IEEE Access, 2021) — plus multivariate statistical evaluation of urban transportation networks (IEEE Access, 2021).
